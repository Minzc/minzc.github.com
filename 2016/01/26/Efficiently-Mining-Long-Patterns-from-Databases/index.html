Efficiently Mining Long Patterns from Databases

Posted on 2016-01-26   |  

Efficiently Mining Long Patterns from Databases
Roberto J. Bayardo Jr.

Abstract

The author presents an algorithm that is used to mining maximal patterns. Tha algorithm adopts look-a-head technique to avoid generating too many mid size patterns. Another contribution of the paper is that they describe a general technique that can be used to obtain a lower-bound on the support of an itemset by exploiting the support information of provided by its subset.

Search space pruning

Look a head

The author represents every node in the enumeration tree by two itemsets, $h(g)$ and $t(g)$. $h(g)$ is the itemset enumerated by the node. The second itemset $t(g)$ is an ordered set and contains all items not in $h(g)$ but could be pontentially appear in any sub-node.

If $h(g) \cup t(g)$ is frequent, then all subsets of $h(g) \cup t(g)$ are frequent but they can not be maximal patterns. Therefore, we cab halt the the sub-node expansion.

Prune Infrequent Items

If $h(g) \cup {i}$, $i \in t(g)$ is infrequent, then any head of a sub-node containing $i$ will be infrequent.

Lower Bound Estimation

Let $sup(I)$ be the set of transactions containing itemset $I$ and $drop(I, i)$ be the set of transactions dropped when we expand $I$ by $i$.

$$drop(I, i) = sup(I) - sup(I \cup i)$$

Therefore,

$$sup(I \cup i) = sup(I)- drop(I, i)$$

If we can get an upper bound of $drop(I, i)$, then we can achieve the lower bound of $sup(I \cup i)$.

Support Lower-Bounding

$drop(I_{s}, i)$ is an upperbound of $drop(I, i)$ where $I_{s}$ is a proper subset of $I$.

We can calculate the lower bound of $I \cup T$ by applying the theorem. $T$ is an itemset disjoint from $I$ and $I_{s}$

$$sup(I) - \sum_{i\in T} drop(I_{s}, i)$$

Using the above theorem, we are able to calculate the upperbound of $h(g) \cup t(g)$
